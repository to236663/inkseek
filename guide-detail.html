Tattoo Aftercare - The Do's and Don'ts

Introduction

Getting a new tattoo is exciting, but the healing period is just as important as the time you spend in the artist's chair. Proper aftercare determines whether your tattoo will stay crisp and vibrant or heal unevenly and lose detail.

Follow Your Artist's Instructions

The best thing you can do is follow your artist's specific instructions, because every artist has preferences based on their technique, equipment, and experience. No matter the design, you should always keep your fresh tattoo clean by gently washing it twice a day with lukewarm water and a mild, fragrance-free soap.

  • Wash with lukewarm water and fragrance-free soap
  • Pat dry with a clean paper towel, avoiding rubbing
  • Apply a thin layer of unscented moisturizer or tattoo aftercare balm
  • Don't over-apply moisture - it can suffocate the area

What to Avoid During Healing

During the healing phase, wearing loose clothing makes a big difference, especially if your tattoo is in a spot where fabric rubs against the skin. Tight clothes can stick to a healing tattoo or cause friction that disrupts the healing process.

  • Don't pick or scratch at the tattoo, even when it itches or flakes
  • Avoid soaking in water - no baths, pools, hot tubs, or beach trips
  • Stay away from sun exposure - UV rays fade tattoos quickly
  • Don't wear tight clothing that creates friction
  • Once healed, sunscreen becomes your tattoo's best friend

The Healing Timeline

It's completely normal for your tattoo to look a bit dull or cloudy during the first couple of weeks. The healing process involves a thin layer of skin forming over the tattoo, temporarily muting the colors or shading. The final appearance usually comes through after the tattoo has fully healed, which generally takes two to four weeks depending on size, placement, and your skin type.

By giving your tattoo the care it needs early on, you're setting it up to stay bold, sharp, and beautiful for years to come.
